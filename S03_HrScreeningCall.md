## 📞 SECTION 3: HR SCREENING CALL – EXACT SCRIPTS

### Typical HR Screening Flow

| Step | Stage | Duration |
|-----|------|---------|
| 1 | HR introduction and company overview | ~2 minutes |
| 2 | "Tell me about yourself" | ~60 seconds |
| 3 | Questions about your background | 5–10 minutes |
| 4 | Questions about the role | ~5 minutes |
| 5 | Salary expectations discussion | 3–5 minutes |
| 6 | Your questions to HR | ~5 minutes |
| 7 | Discussion about next steps | ~2 minutes |

**Total Call Duration:** 20–30 minutes

---

## HR Question 1: Why Did You Choose BCA?

### Suggested Response

> I chose **BCA** because I've always been interested in how technology shapes our daily lives. During my **12th standard**, I was deciding between a pure IT path and something that also included business applications. BCA offered the perfect combination of both.
>
> The three-year program exposed me to areas like **programming, databases, web development, and system analysis**, which helped me build a solid technical foundation.
>
> More importantly, it helped me develop **problem-solving and logical thinking skills**, which are essential for any technology-related role, including customer support and technical troubleshooting.
>
> Looking back, I feel it was the right decision because it has prepared me well to **enter the tech industry and adapt to different technical roles.**

---

| Practice Method | Details |
|-----------------|---------|
| Repetition | Practice 3–4 times daily |

| Focus | Speak clearly and confidently |
| Tip | Emphasize learning and career alignment |

### 🎯 What the Interviewer Wants to Know

| Aspect | What They Are Checking |
|------|------------------------|
| Decision-making | Why you chose this field |

| Career direction | Whether your education supports your goals |

## 📞 HR Question 2: You're a Fresher With No Work Experience. Why Should We Hire You?

### Suggested Response

> That's a fair question. While I may not have formal work experience yet, I believe I bring **three strong qualities** that make me a good candidate for this role.
>
> **First, my relevant academic background.** Through my BCA degree, I’ve developed a solid understanding of technology, troubleshooting, and logical problem-solving. This helps me quickly understand technical issues that customers might face.
>
> **Second, my ability to learn quickly.** During my BCA, I had to learn multiple technologies within limited timeframes—such as programming languages, databases, and web development tools. This experience shows that I can quickly learn your CRM systems, internal tools, and product knowledge.
>
> **Third, my attitude and people skills.** I’m patient, empathetic, and detail-oriented, and I genuinely enjoy helping people solve problems. These soft skills are essential for customer support and help create a positive experience for customers.
>
> While I may be starting my professional career, I’m highly motivated, eager to learn, and ready to put in the effort to quickly grow into the role and contribute effectively to the team.

---

### 📝 Practice Guide

| Practice Method | Details |
|-----------------|---------|
| Repetitions | Practice 3–4 times daily |
| Ideal Length | 40–60 seconds |
| Focus | Confidence and clarity |
| Tip | Emphasize your strengths rather than the lack of experience |

---

### 🎯 Structure of This Answer

| Step | What You Do |
|-----|--------------|
| 1 | Acknowledge the concern honestly |
| 2 | Present 3 strengths |
| 3 | Connect them to the role |
| 4 | Show motivation and willingness to learn |

---

### 💡 Why This Answer Works

| Element | What It Shows |
|--------|---------------|
| Honesty | You acknowledge you're a fresher |
| Skills | Academic knowledge relevant to the role |
| Learning Ability | Fast adaptation to tools and processes |
| Attitude | Positive mindset and willingness to grow |
