# 📞 SECTION 3: HR SCREENING CALL – EXACT SCRIPTS

## Typical HR Screening Flow

| Step | Stage | Description | Duration |
|-----|------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | HR Introduction | HR introduces themselves and explains the company | ~2 mins |
| 2 | Self Introduction | "Tell me about yourself" | ~1 min |
| 3 | Background Questions | Education, skills, experience | 5–10 mins |
| 4 | Role Discussion | Questions about customer support | ~5 mins |
| 5 | Salary Discussion | Expectations and compensation | 3–5 mins |
| 6 | Your Questions | You ask HR questions | ~5 mins |
| 7 | Next Steps | HR explains next interview stage | ~2 mins |

**Total Call Duration:** 20–30 minutes

---

# HR Q1: Why Did You Choose BCA?

### Suggested Response

"I chose BCA because I've always been fascinated by how technology shapes our daily lives. In 12th standard, I was deciding between pure IT and business applications, and BCA provided the perfect combination of both.

The program covered programming, databases, web development, and system analysis, which gave me a strong technical foundation.

More importantly, it helped me develop problem-solving and logical thinking skills, which are important for technology roles, including customer support.

Looking back, I feel it was the right choice because it has prepared me well to transition into the tech industry."

---

# HR Q2: You're a Fresher With No Work Experience. Why Should We Hire You?

### Suggested Response

"That's a fair question. While I don't have formal work experience yet, I bring three important strengths.

First, my academic background. Through my BCA degree, I've developed strong technical understanding, troubleshooting ability, and logical problem-solving skills.

Second, my ability to learn quickly. During my BCA, I learned multiple technologies in short timeframes such as programming languages, databases, and web development. This shows I can quickly learn your CRM tools and internal processes.

Third, my attitude and soft skills. I'm patient, empathetic, detail-oriented, and genuinely enjoy helping people solve problems.

While I may be a fresher, I'm highly motivated and ready to work hard to grow quickly and contribute to the team."

---

# HR Q3: What Do You Know About Customer Support?

### Suggested Response

"Customer Support is the frontline interface between a company and its customers. The main goal is to help customers solve problems, answer their questions, and ensure they have a positive experience with the product or service.

From my research, customer support typically includes:

- Responding to customer queries through email, chat, or phone  
- Troubleshooting technical or non-technical issues  
- Documenting customer issues for product or engineering teams  
- Escalating complex problems to technical specialists  
- Following up with customers to ensure satisfaction  
- Meeting key performance metrics like response time, resolution time, and CSAT

Modern customer support teams also use CRM platforms such as Zendesk or Freshdesk and rely heavily on strong communication skills.

I'm excited to learn the practical aspects and develop expertise in this field."

---

# HR Q4: Can You Work Night Shifts?

## Option A — If You Can Work Night Shifts

"Yes, I'm comfortable working night shifts. I understand that companies serving global customers often require this schedule.

I'm flexible and able to adjust my routine accordingly. I also understand that night shifts sometimes include additional shift allowances.

I would just like to understand the shift rotation policy — whether shifts are fixed or rotational — and what support the company provides for night-shift employees."

---

## Option B — If You Prefer Day Shifts

"I'm primarily looking for day shifts because I find I'm most productive during regular working hours.

However, I understand that customer support sometimes requires flexibility. If occasional night shifts are necessary, I'm open to discussing them, especially during training or important situations.

Could you share how frequently night shifts are required for this role?"

---

# HR Q5: What Are Your Salary Expectations?

### Suggested Response

"Thank you for asking. Based on my research for customer support roles in the industry, especially for remote or hybrid positions, and considering my BCA background, I'm looking at a range of **₹3.5 to ₹4.5 LPA**.

However, I'm flexible and would like to understand the complete compensation structure, including performance bonuses, health benefits, and growth opportunities.

The overall learning and career development matter to me as much as the salary.

May I know what the typical salary range is for this role at your company?"

---

### Why This Answer Works

- Anchors the salary range (₹3.5–4.5 LPA)
- Shows market research
- Demonstrates flexibility
- Focuses on overall growth
- Invites HR to share their range

# HR Q6: Do You Have Other Offers or Interviews?

## Option A — If You Have Other Applications

"I'm currently exploring a few other opportunities in the customer support space because I want to find the right fit for my first job.

However, your company is definitely one of my top choices because of your product, company culture, and the opportunity to grow in a remote or hybrid environment."

---

## Option B — If This Is Your Only Application

"I'm being selective with where I apply because I want to start my career in a company where I can grow long-term.

Your company stood out to me because of the product, work culture, and growth opportunities, so I prioritized applying here."

⚠️ **Always be honest. Never lie about offers.**

# HR Q8: Why Are You Looking for a Remote/Hybrid Role?

### Suggested Response

"I prefer a remote or hybrid setup for three main reasons.

First, productivity. Working in a focused environment helps me concentrate better and deliver quality work.

Second, flexibility. A hybrid model allows me to collaborate with the team when needed while maintaining a healthy work-life balance.

Third, it aligns with modern work culture. Remote work requires strong time management, discipline, and communication skills, which I want to develop early in my career.

That said, I'm completely open to coming to the office whenever required for meetings, training, or collaboration."
