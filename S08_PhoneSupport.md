# 📞 SECTION 8: Phone Support Scripts

---

# ☎️ Phone Support Basics

Great phone support is about **clarity, empathy, and professionalism**.

### Key Guidelines

✅ Answer within **3 rings**  
✅ **Smile while talking** (customers can hear it in your voice)  
✅ Speak **clearly and at a moderate pace**  
✅ Use the **customer's name 2–3 times** during the call  
✅ **Take notes** while the customer explains the issue  
✅ **Confirm resolution** before ending the call  
✅ Always **thank the customer for calling**

---

# 👋 Phone Opening Script

You:  
"Good **[morning/afternoon]**, thank you for calling **[Company Name] support**!  
My name is **[Your First Name]**. May I have your name please?"

Customer:  
[Provides name]

You:  
"Thank you, **[Customer Name]**! How can I help you today?"

Customer explains issue.

You:  
"I understand. So you're experiencing **[summarize the issue in your own words]**.  
I'm sorry you're facing this problem. Let me help resolve this for you right away.

May I have your **[account number / email / order number]** so I can pull up your information?"

---

# 🎧 Active Listening Phrases

Use these to show empathy and understanding.

- "I see..."
- "I understand..."
- "That makes sense..."
- "I can imagine how frustrating that must be..."
- "Let me make sure I understand correctly..."  
- "Thank you for explaining that..."

---

# ⏸️ Putting a Customer on Hold

### ❌ Wrong Approach

"Hold please."  
*(Immediately puts customer on hold)*

---

### ✅ Correct Approach

You:  
"[Customer Name], I need to check something in our system to assist you with this.

Would it be okay if I place you on a brief hold for **about 2 minutes**?"

Customer:  
"Okay"

You:  
"Thank you! I'll be right back with you."

*(Place customer on hold — set a 2-minute timer)*

---

### Returning from Hold

You:  
"Thank you so much for holding, **[Customer Name]**!  
I really appreciate your patience. Here's what I found..."

---

### If It Takes Longer

After 2 minutes:

You:  
"Hi **[Customer Name]**, thank you for holding!  
I'm still checking on this and it's taking a bit longer than expected.

Would you prefer to **hold for another 2–3 minutes**, or would you like me to **call you back in about 15 minutes** once I have the information?"

---

# 🔁 Transferring a Call

### ❌ Wrong Approach

"That's not my department. Let me transfer you."

*(Blind transfer without context)*

---

### ✅ Correct Approach

You:  
"[Customer Name], I want to make sure you get the best assistance for this issue.

This requires help from our **[Technical / Billing / Specialist] team**.  
I'll transfer you to them and **brief them on your issue** so you don't have to repeat everything.

Is that okay?"

Customer:  
"Yes"

You:  
"Perfect! Your **reference number is [12345]**.  
Please hold while I transfer your call."

---

### Warm Transfer (Internal Communication)

You to Next Agent:

"Hi **[Agent Name]**, I have **[Customer Name]** on the line regarding **[brief issue summary]**.  
Their account number is **[account number]** and they have already tried **[troubleshooting steps]**.  
Could you take it from here?"

Next Agent:  
"Yes, got it."

You to Customer:

"[Customer Name], I'm transferring you to **[Agent Name]** now.  
They are fully briefed on your situation. Thank you for calling!"

---

# 🏁 Phone Closing Script

You:  
"Alright **[Customer Name]**, just to recap what we did today:

✅ **[Action 1]**  
✅ **[Action 2]**  
✅ **[Action 3]**

Is everything working properly now?"

Customer:  
"Yes, thank you!"

You:  
"Wonderful! I'm glad I could help.  
Is there anything else I can assist you with today?"

Customer:  
"No, that's all."

You:  
"Perfect! Thank you for calling **[Company Name]**, **[Customer Name]**.  
Have a great day!"

*(Always wait for the customer to hang up first.)*

---

# ⚠️ Handling Difficult Phone Calls

## 😡 Angry Customer

Customer:  
*(Yelling / complaining)*

**Do not interrupt. Let them vent for 30–45 seconds.**

You:

"[Customer Name], I sincerely apologize for this frustration.  
I understand you're upset, and you have every right to feel that way.

I'm here to make this right for you. Let me see what I can do immediately."

After providing solution:

"I've **[action taken]**.

Additionally, I'm **[compensation / extra support]** to help resolve this issue.

I want to make sure you're satisfied with this resolution.  
Does this work for you?"

---

## 🚫 Abusive or Threatening Customer

You:

"[Customer Name], I truly want to help you, but I need us to maintain a respectful conversation.

If the language continues, I may have to end the call.  
I'm here to solve your problem — can we work together on this?"

If abuse continues:

You:

"I'm sorry, but I'm unable to continue this call under these circumstances.

I'm escalating this issue to my supervisor, who will contact you within **2 hours**.

Your reference number is **[12345]**.

Goodbye."

*(End call and immediately report to supervisor.)*
