# 💰 SECTION 4: Salary Negotiation – Word-for-Word Scripts

## 🎯 Your Salary Strategy

**Profile:** BCA Final Year, Fresher, No Experience

| Type | Amount | Meaning |
|-----|-----|-----|
| Minimum Acceptable | ₹3 LPA | Lowest salary you will accept |
| Target Range | ₹3.5 – 4.5 LPA | What you should ask for |

# SCENARIO 1: HR Asks Salary Expectations

**HR:**  
"What are your salary expectations for this role?"

**YOU:**

> "Thank you for asking. Based on my research for Customer Support roles in the industry — especially for remote and hybrid positions with companies similar to yours — and considering my BCA background, technical aptitude, and strong communication skills in English and Hindi, I'm looking at a range of ₹3.5 to 4.5 LPA annually.  
>
> However, I'm flexible and would love to first understand the complete compensation package you offer — the base salary, performance bonuses, health insurance, paid leaves, and other benefits.  
>
> Could you share what the typical range is for this position at [Company Name]?"

**PAUSE. LET THEM RESPOND.**

---

# SCENARIO 2: They Offer ₹3 LPA (Below Your Target)

**HR:**  
"We're offering ₹3 LPA for this role."

**YOU (Polite Pushback):**

> "Thank you for sharing that. I really appreciate the offer and I'm genuinely interested in [Company Name] because of [specific reason — product, remote culture, team].
>
> However, I was hoping for something closer to ₹3.5–4 LPA based on industry standards for customer support roles with technical backgrounds.
>
> I understand I'm a fresher, but my BCA degree gives me a strong technical foundation that can add value — I can troubleshoot technical issues faster and communicate technical concepts clearly to customers.
>
> Is there any flexibility in the salary? Or could we explore other components like performance bonuses or a faster salary review cycle after 6 months?"

---

## If They Say: “₹3 LPA is Final”

### OPTION A — Negotiate Other Benefits

> "I understand the constraints for fresher salaries. I really want to work with [Company Name], so let me ask — is there flexibility in other areas?
>
> For example:
>
> - Could we schedule a performance review after **6 months instead of 12 months**?
> - Is there a **joining bonus** available?
> - Could we add **extra paid leaves** or work-from-home flexibility?
> - Are there any **certifications or training programs** the company sponsors?"

---

### OPTION B — Accept but Set Future Expectation

> "I understand. I really value this opportunity and I can see myself growing with [Company Name]. I'm willing to accept ₹3 LPA to get started.
>
> However, I would like to request a **salary review at the 6-month mark based on my performance** rather than waiting a full year. Would that be possible?"

---

### If They Say: "We can offer ₹3.2 LPA"

**YOU:**

> "Thank you for working with me on this. ₹3.2 LPA works for me, and I really appreciate your flexibility. I'm excited to join the team and contribute to [Company Name]'s success.
>
> What are the next steps in the process?"

---

# SCENARIO 3: They Offer ₹3.5 LPA

**HR:**  
"We can offer you ₹3.5 LPA."

### OPTION A — Accept Happily

> "That's wonderful, thank you! ₹3.5 LPA aligns well with my expectations. I'm really excited about this opportunity and ready to get started.
>
> Could you share the complete offer details including salary breakdown and benefits?"

---

### OPTION B — Try Slightly Higher

> "Thank you for the offer of ₹3.5 LPA. Before I confirm, could I ask if there is any possibility to move closer to **₹3.8–4 LPA**?
>
> If ₹3.5 LPA is the final number, I completely understand and I'm still very interested."

---

### If They Say: "We can do ₹3.7 LPA"

> "Perfect! ₹3.7 LPA works excellently for me. Thank you for being flexible. I'm looking forward to joining the team."

---

# SCENARIO 4: They Offer ₹4 LPA+

**HR:**  
"We'd like to offer you ₹4 LPA."

**YOU:**

> "That's fantastic! ₹4 LPA aligns perfectly with my expectations. I'm really excited about this opportunity and grateful for the offer.
>
> Could you please send me the formal offer letter with all the details?"

**Do NOT negotiate further. Accept gracefully.**

---

# SCENARIO 5: HR Asks Current Salary (Fresher Trap)

**HR:**  
"What is your current salary?"

**YOU:**

> "I'm currently a full-time BCA student, so I don't have a previous salary. This will be my first full-time role.  
>
> That's why I based my expectations on **market research for customer support roles** rather than a previous salary."

---

# SCENARIO 6: Low Offer With Future Promise

**HR:**  
"Start at ₹2.5 LPA. After 6 months we'll increase to ₹3.5 LPA."

**YOU:**

> "I appreciate the transparency. However I'm hesitant about starting below ₹3 LPA even with a future promise.
>
> If the increment to ₹3.5 LPA after 6 months can be **included in the offer letter**, I would be comfortable starting at ₹2.5 LPA."

---

### If They Say: “We can't put it in writing”

**YOU:**

> "In that case, would it be possible to start at **₹3 LPA** instead? That would make it easier for me to commit without uncertainty."

---

# 📄 Getting Everything in Writing

After the verbal offer is finalized:

**YOU SAY:**

> "Thank you again for the offer! I'm excited to move forward.  
>
> Could you please send the formal offer letter including:
>
> - Base Salary (₹X LPA)
> - Performance Bonuses
> - Health Insurance
> - Paid Leaves
> - Shift Timings / Shift Allowances
> - Probation Period
> - Notice Period
> - Joining Date
> - Work Location (Remote / Hybrid)
> - Other Benefits
>
> Once I review the offer letter, I'll sign and return it promptly."

---

# ⭐ Golden Rules for Salary Negotiation

1️⃣ Always anchor higher than your minimum  
→ Ask **₹3.5–4.5 LPA**, not ₹3 LPA  

2️⃣ Never accept the first offer immediately  

3️⃣ Always ask about **total compensation**  

4️⃣ Be polite but confident  

5️⃣ Know your walk-away point  
→ **Below ₹3 LPA = consider declining**

6️⃣ Get everything **in writing**

7️⃣ Don't negotiate forever  
→ 1–2 rounds max

8️⃣ Always end on a positive note
